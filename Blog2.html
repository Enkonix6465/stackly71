Modern Urban Residence

A stunning transformation of a traditional home into a contemporary urban sanctuary

By Michael Anderson
June 15, 2024
12 min read

Project Overview

This project involved the complete transformation of a 1980s suburban home into a modern urban residence that seamlessly blends contemporary design with functional living. Located in a rapidly developing neighborhood, the home now stands as a testament to innovative architectural solutions that maximize space, light, and connection to the outdoors.

12
Months Completion
3,200
Square Feet
$850K
Project Value

1. Design Philosophy & Approach

Client Vision & Requirements

The homeowners sought to transform their outdated property into a modern, energy-efficient home that would serve as both a family sanctuary and an entertainment space. Key requirements included open-plan living, abundant natural light, sustainable materials, and seamless indoor-outdoor connectivity.

  • Maximize natural light throughout the home
  • Create flexible living spaces for family and entertainment
  • Incorporate sustainable and low-maintenance materials
  • Establish strong connection to the garden and outdoor areas
  • Implement smart home technology seamlessly

Design Solutions

  • Expansive floor-to-ceiling windows and skylights
  • Open-plan kitchen, dining and living area
  • Bamboo flooring and recycled glass countertops
  • Bi-fold doors opening to extended patio
  • Integrated smart lighting and climate control

2. Key Architectural Features

Transformative Elements

Double-Height Living Space

The removal of a section of the first floor created a dramatic double-height living area that floods the space with natural light and creates a sense of grandeur.

Green Roof System

A section of the roof was transformed into a green space with native plants, providing insulation, managing stormwater, and creating a private outdoor retreat.

Cantilevered First Floor Extension

A bold architectural statement that added valuable square footage without compromising the garden space below, creating a covered outdoor entertaining area.

3. Material Selection & Sustainability

Sustainable Materials

We prioritized environmentally responsible materials that offer durability, low maintenance, and aesthetic appeal while minimizing ecological impact.

  • • Bamboo flooring from sustainable sources
  • • Recycled glass countertops
  • • Low-VOC paints and finishes
  • • Reclaimed timber features

Energy Efficiency

The home incorporates multiple energy-saving features that reduce environmental impact while providing comfort and cutting utility costs.

  • • Solar panel array on south-facing roof
  • • High-performance triple-glazed windows
  • • Advanced insulation throughout
  • • Energy recovery ventilation system

Water Management

Innovative water systems were implemented to reduce consumption and manage stormwater effectively on the urban site.

  • • Greywater recycling system
  • • Rainwater harvesting for irrigation
  • • Permeable paving in driveway
  • • Drought-resistant native planting

4. Interior Spaces & Layout

Thoughtfully Designed Living Areas

Ground Floor Layout

  • Open-plan kitchen, dining and living area (850 sq ft)
  • Guest bedroom with ensuite bathroom
  • Butler's pantry and utility room
  • Integrated garage with EV charging

First Floor Layout

Master Suite 400 sq ft

Walk-in wardrobe, luxury bathroom, private balcony

Children's Bedrooms 300 sq ft each

Two bedrooms sharing a Jack-and-Jill bathroom

Home Office 200 sq ft

Sound-insulated with built-in shelving

5. Construction Process & Challenges

1

Site Preparation & Demolition

The existing structure required careful deconstruction with 85% of materials salvaged for reuse or recycling. Unexpected foundation issues were discovered and addressed with helical piers to stabilize the structure.

Challenge Overcome:

Original foundation required additional reinforcement, adding 3 weeks to the timeline but ensuring long-term structural integrity.

2

Structural Framework

The cantilevered extension required precise engineering and specialized steelwork. Collaboration between architects and structural engineers resulted in an innovative solution that minimized support columns.

3

Envelope & Systems Installation

High-performance building envelope with advanced insulation and air sealing. Integration of mechanical systems including HVAC, plumbing, and electrical with smart home infrastructure.

4

Interior Finishes & Landscaping

Installation of sustainable material finishes and custom cabinetry. Simultaneous exterior landscaping with native plants and permeable surfaces to manage stormwater runoff.

Creating exceptional architectural experiences through innovative design and quality construction.
